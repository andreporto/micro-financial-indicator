System Documentation

Software Engineering Specification, Algorithms and Data Sources

Multi-layer Data Architecture

The SaaS operates in a modular structure that separates chart rendering, indicator statistical calculations, and integration with high-latency on-chain APIs.

1. Data Collection

Incremental runtime fetch via CORS-enabled APIs for Binance (spot market). Support for daily data (1d) to reconstruct complex timeframes.

2. Aggregation & Processing

Client-side and server-side mechanisms consolidate daily candles into 3d and 2w periods, eliminating dependency on restricted exchange endpoints.

3. Calculation Engine

Dedicated mathematical modules to compute fast and slow moving averages, oscillators, structural imbalance limits (FVGs), and S/R pivots.

High Probability Divergence Detection

Divergences on the **Stochastic RSI** in 3d and 1w timeframes provide excellent macro triggers for trend exhaustion.

Pivot Identification Logic:

The system monitors local price action and momentum curves. A bullish divergence occurs if price creates lower lows ($Low_2 < Low_1$) while the fast line of Stoch RSI (%K) generates higher lows ($Stoch\_K_2 > Stoch\_K_1$), provided at least one of the lows occurs below the sell exhaustion limit ($Stoch\_K \le 20$).

Market Structure: Fair Value Gaps and S/R Zones

Fair Value Gaps measure the lack of liquidity on the chart. The price has a historical tendency to rebalance these areas.

FVG Detection (3-Candle Sequence)

  • Bullish FVG: Candle 3 Low > Candle 1 High. The gap is treated as a support zone for buys.
  • Bearish FVG: Candle 3 High < Candle 1 Low. The gap is treated as a resistance zone for sells.

Support and Resistance Zones by Retest Clustering

Historical pivots are scored by sweeping the entire time series and counting how many retests occurred near the pivot price. Levels are consolidated by excluding nearby duplicates and sorted based on current price proximity, returning the 3 strongest active zones.

Fundamental On-Chain Confluence

Network metrics confirm whether the price is cheap or expensive from a utility value and average mining/acquisition cost perspective.

Metric Decision-Making Purpose
STH Realized Price (STH-RP) Acts as the average purchase price of recent investors. Dynamic support during healthy uptrends.
LTH Realized Price (LTH-RP) Average cost of "strong hands". Marks the historical final capitulation level in bear markets.
MVRV Z-Score Identifies market extremes in standard deviations. Z-Score below 0.2 is a macro buying opportunity.
Rainbow Chart Frames the current price into long-term logarithmic growth curves to determine the greed level.
